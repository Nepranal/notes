# Method

It's also quite similar to 1.11 but this time we need to find a way to draw our triangles:

![alt text](<../sources/The Elements/Screenshot 2025-12-23 at 2.09.28â€¯AM.png>)

The construction has $CG=CE$. We then draw a line $CH$ such that it bisects the angle at $C$ (proposition 1.9). Now, since we have 2 triangles $\triangle GCH, \triangle CEH$ and $CG=CE$, with $\angle GCH = \angle ECH$, by proposition 1.4, the two triangles must be equivalent. This results to $\angle CHG = \angle CHE$.
