# Proposition

> If two straight-lines cut one another then they make the vertically opposite angles equal to one another.

# Proof

![alt text](<../sources/The Elements/Screenshot 2025-12-23 at 11.38.28â€¯PM.png>)

Say $\angle AED = \theta$ and $\angle AEC = \phi$. We have to show that $\angle CEB = \theta, \angle DEB = \phi$.

By proposition 1.13, we can say that $\phi = \pi - \theta$. Since $\angle DEB$ is also adjacent to $\theta$, then $\angle DEB = \pi - \theta = \theta$. You can do this one more time to show that $\angle CEB = \theta$.
